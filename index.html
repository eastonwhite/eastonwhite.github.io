I am a quantitative ecologist who uses mathematical and statistical tools, coupled with experiments and field observations, to solve problems in ecology, conservation, sustainability, and ecosystem management. I am currently a Research Associate in the Department of Biology at the University of Vermont. I am the instructor for Foundations of Quantitative Reasoning, a PhD course at the University of Vermont. The class is a mix of ecology, evolution, quantitative skills, and programming in R. In my role at UVM, I am also on the leadership team for a NSF-funded PhD traineeship focused on quantitative skills, interdisiplinary work, as well as diversity and inclusion.

I currently conduct research on assessing the effectiveness of protected area networks, improving species monitoring programs, and modeling socio-ecological systems in the context of fisheries.
